INTRODUCTION
We can define motion as the change of position of an object with respect to time
A book falling off a table, water flowing from the tap,rattling windows, etc all exhibit motion.
Even the air that we breathe exhibits motion! Everything in the universe moves. We live in a universe that is incontinual motion.
2.1
Rest and Motion
Motion   - Change in position of an object with respect to its surroundings.
Examples:  
  • Children playing in the ground
  • Birds flying in the sky
  • Cars, Buses and bikes moving on road
  • Movement of earth around sun
Rest   -There is no change in position of an object with respect to its surroundings.
2.2
Types of Motion
2.2.1
Uniform & Non Uniform motion
Types of motion
Linear motion
- Motion along straight line
Circular motion
- Motion along a circular path
Oscillatory motion
- To and fro motion of an object
Random motion
- Move in any possible way (not uniform)
Uniform motion -An object covers equal distance in equal intervals of time
Example :-
  • A car covering 60km in first, second , third andfourth hour
  • Clock, Rotating fan
Non-uniform motion- An object covers unequaldistance in equal intervals of time

2.3
Distance and Displacement
2.3.1
Distance

Distance:-Actual length of the path travelled by an moving object.

SI Unit:-Metre

* It is a scalar quantity having magnitude only.

2.3.2
Displacement

Displacement: - Change in position of an object with respect to direction

SI Unit:-Metre

* It is a vector quantity having both magnitude and direction

2.4
Speed, Velocity and Acceleration
2.4.1
Speed

Speed: -Rate of change of distance (or) distance covered in unit time.

S.I Unit:-Metre/Second (or) ms1

* It is a Scalar Quantity

Speed =Distance travelled/time taken
2.4.2
Velocity

Velocity(v) -Rate of change of displacement with respect to time

S.I Unit:-ms-1

*It is a vector quantity. (Hint:     -displacement has direction)

*Velocity = Displacement / Time taken

Distance = 20 metres

2.4.3
Acceleration

Acceleration(a) - Rate of change of velocity in unit time

     = change in velocity / time-1

     = [Final velocity(v) - Initial velocity(u)] / time

Positive acceleration-v > u (Final velocity greater than final velocity)

Negative acceleration-u > v (Initial velocity greater than final velocity)

2.8
Uniform circular motion
When an object is moving with a constant speed along a circular path, the velocity changes dueto the change in direction. Hence, it is an accelerated motion.
For example, revolution of earth around the sun,revolution of moon around the earth
If an object, moving along a circular path of radius, r takes time, T to come back to its startingposition, then the speed, v is given by,
Speed = Circumference/Time taken
V = 2pr/T
2.9
Centripetal Acceleration and Centripetal Force
When the acceleration is directed inwards/to the centrein a circular motion, it is known as centripetal acceleration
For an object of mass m, moving along a circular
path of radius r, with a velocity v.
Its centripetal acceleration is given by
a = v2/r
Magnitude (F) = Mass x Centripetal Acceleration
F = mv2/r
2.10
Centrifugal Force
When the acceleration is directed outwards/ away from the centrein a circular motion, it is known as centrifugal acceleration and the associated force is centrifugal force
