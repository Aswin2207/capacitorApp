INTRODUCTION
All substances in our surrounding are made up of molecules. These molecules are generally at motion and posses kineticenergy. The sum of the kinetic and potential energy is called the internal energy of the molecules.
Heat energy–Outward Flow of internal energy (Internal energy–Potential energy + kinetic energy)

Expansion:   when heat is applied, the molecules gain energy and vibrate and force other molecules apart
Change in State:   When heat is applied , Solid changes to Liquid, Liquid to Vapor and Vapor to Gas.
Change in Temperature:   When heat is applied, the increase in kinetic energy makes the particles to move rapidly and temperature rises

Radiation in Daily life (More Examples)
Heat does not stay where we put it. Hot things get colder andcold things get hotter.
Heat is transferred from one place to another till theirtemperatures become equal.
Heat transfer takes place when heat energy flows from anobject with higher temperature to an object with lowertemperature
Heat transfer takes place in three ways:
  1. Conduction
  2. Convection
  3. Radiation
The process of transfer of heat in solids from a region of highertemperature to a region of lower temperature without the actualmovement of molecules is called conduction
Conduction is the process by which heat energy is transmittedthrough collisions between neighbouring atoms or molecules.
Conduction occurs more readily in solids and liquids, where theparticles are closer together than in gases, where particles arefurther apart. The rate of energy transfer by conduction is higherwhen there is a large temperature difference between thesubstances that are in contact
  1. Metals are good conductors of heat. So, aluminumis used for making utensils to cook food quickly.
  2. Mercury is used in thermometers because, it is a good conductor of heat.
  3. We wear woolen clothes is winter to keep ourselves warm. Air, which is a bad conductor, doesnot allow our body heat to escape..
Convection is the flow of heat through a fluid from places ofhigher temperature to places of lower temperature bymovement of the fluid itself.
Convection occurs when a cooler, denser mass of gas or liquidreplaces warmer, less dense mass of gas or liquid by pushingit upward
When a fluid is continuously heated, the warmer fluid movesupward away from the heat source, it cools down, increasesin density, and sinks into the warmer, less dense fluid below.This process repeats itself, resulting in a continuousconvection current
  • Boiling Water-When water boils, the heat passes fromthe burner into the pot, heating the water at the bottom.This hot water rises, and cooler water moves down toreplace it, causing a circular motion
  • Steaming cup of hot tea-The steam you see whendrinking a cup of hot tea indicates that heat is beingtransferred into the air.
  • Ice melting-Ice melts because heat moves to the ice fromthe air. As a result, the ice melts from a solid to liquid
  • Hot air balloons:Air molecules at the bottom of theballoon get heated by a heat source and rise. As the warmair rises, cold air is pushed downward and it is alsoheated. When the hot air is trapped inside the balloon, itrises
Radiation is the flow of heat from one place to another bymeans of electromagnetic waves.
Transfer of heat energy from the sun reaches us in the formof radiation
Heat transfer takes place in three ways:
  1. White or light colored cloths are good reflectors of heat. They keep us cool during summer.
  2. Base of cooking utensils is blackened because black surface absorbs more heat from the surrounding.
  3. Surface of airplane is highly polished because it helps to reflect most of the heat radiation from the sun
  • Sun-Most of the radiation emitted by the Sun isabsorbed by the atmosphere. However, the part which isnot absorbed by the atmosphere reaches the earth.Humans are exposed to this part of the radiation almostall times.
  • Mobile Phones-The exposure to radiofrequencyradiation causes heating of the area of the body where acell phone is being held like near the ear. However, theamount of heat radiated is not sufficient to increase thetemperature of the body.
  • Laser Beam-As the name indicates, Light Amplification Stimulated Emission of Radiation also produces radiation.

LATENT HEAT
Latent heat means hidden heat or hidden Energy
Latent heat is the amount of heat energy absorbed or released by a substance during a change in its physical states without any change in its temperature.
Specific latent heat is the amount of heat energy absorbed or liberated by unit mass of a substance during change of state without causing any change in temperature.
L=Q/m
The SI unit of specific latent heat is J/kg.

SPECIFIC HEAT CAPACITY
Specific heat capacity of a substance is defined as the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of 1 kg of the substance by 10C or 1 K
SI unit of specific heat capacity is Jkg-1K-1
A high heat capacity means that the substance requires lots of heat in order toraise or lower the temperature by one unit. Having a high heat capacity meansthat the substance also retains heat better than substances with lower heat capacities
A low heat capacity means that the substance does not require a lot of heat inorder to raise/lower the temperature by 1°C. A low heat capacity means that thesubstance does not retain heat as well as substances with a higher heat capacity Formula for Specific Heat Capacity
